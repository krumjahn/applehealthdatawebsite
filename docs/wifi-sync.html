iPhone-to-Mac WiFi Sync

Sync your Apple Health data directly from your iPhone to the Mac app over your local WiFi or ethernet network. No export files to manage, no cloud services, and no AirDrop required. Just a fast, private, device-to-device transfer.

Your health data travels directly between your iPhone and Mac on your local network. It never touches the internet.

Requirements

  • iPhone app installed on your iPhone (iOS 16 or later)
  • Mac app installed on your Mac (macOS 13 Ventura or later)
  • Both devices connected to the same local network (WiFi or ethernet)

How It Works

  1. Open the Mac app and navigate to the Sync tab.
  2. The Mac app displays a connection code and its local IP address.
  3. On your iPhone, open the app and tap "Sync to Mac".
  4. Your Mac appears automatically on the local network, or you can enter the connection code manually.
  5. Select which health metrics you want to sync (steps, heart rate, sleep, workouts, and more).
  6. Tap Start Sync. Data transfers directly over the local network to your Mac.

What Gets Synced

WiFi Sync transfers all Apple Health record types, including:

  • Activity -- steps, distance, flights climbed, active energy
  • Heart -- heart rate, resting heart rate, HRV, blood pressure
  • Sleep -- sleep stages, time in bed, sleep duration
  • Workouts -- type, duration, calories, route data
  • Body -- weight, BMI, body fat percentage
  • Nutrition -- dietary energy, macronutrients, water intake
  • Vitals -- blood oxygen, respiratory rate, body temperature
  • Mindfulness -- mindful minutes, state of mind

You can choose to sync everything or select only the categories you need.

Privacy and Security

WiFi Sync is designed with privacy as the top priority:

  • Data is transferred directly between your devices on the local network.
  • No data leaves your network. Nothing is uploaded to any server or cloud service.
  • The connection uses your local network only -- no internet connection is required during the sync.
  • The connection code ensures only your devices can pair.

Menu Bar Sync Status

The Mac app includes a macOS menu bar indicator that shows the current sync status. You can see at a glance whether a sync is in progress, when the last sync completed, and if any errors occurred. Click the menu bar icon to quickly open the Sync tab or trigger a new sync.

Troubleshooting

Mac not appearing on iPhone

  • Confirm both devices are on the same WiFi network or the same ethernet subnet.
  • Check your Mac's firewall settings (System Settings > Network > Firewall). The Health Data Export app must be allowed to accept incoming connections.
  • Try entering the connection code manually instead of relying on automatic discovery.
  • Restart both apps and try again.

Sync is slow

  • Large datasets (several years of health data) may take a few minutes, especially on slower WiFi connections.
  • A 5 GHz WiFi connection will be faster than 2.4 GHz.
  • Ethernet connections provide the most reliable and fastest transfer speeds.

Sync fails or disconnects

  • Make sure neither device goes to sleep during the transfer. Keep the iPhone screen on and prevent the Mac from sleeping.
  • Move closer to your WiFi router if the signal is weak.
  • If the problem persists, try syncing a smaller selection of health categories first.
