Prompt Library

Copy-paste prompts designed for OpenClaw and other AI tools. Each prompt is built to produce consistent, actionable outputs from your local Apple Health dataset. These assume your health export is already imported into the Mac app and the localhost API is running.

Prompt Prefix

Start every OpenClaw prompt with this prefix. It tells OpenClaw to route the request through the local analyzer API on your Mac:

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.

You can omit this prefix if you are pasting prompts directly into ChatGPT, Claude, or another AI tool with your exported CSV/JSON files attached.

Daily Brief

A short morning check-in that covers your key metrics and gives you actionable suggestions for the day.

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
Give me my daily health brief for today and 3 suggestions.

Detailed daily brief

If you want more granular output with specific metric breakdowns:

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
Give me a detailed daily health brief for today. Include:
- Steps vs. my 30-day average
- Sleep duration and bedtime consistency
- Resting heart rate trend (last 7 days)
- Any workouts logged yesterday
- 3 specific, actionable suggestions

Weekly Review

A weekly summary that compares the past seven days against the prior week and produces a scorecard.

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
Pull my daily brief for the last 7 days and summarize trends in steps, sleep, and workouts.
Then give me a weekly scorecard (Consistency, Recovery, Activity) and 3 experiments for next week.

Monthly review

Expand the window to 30 days for a broader trend analysis:

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
Summarize the last 30 days of health data. Compare weeks 1-2 vs. weeks 3-4.
Highlight the biggest improvement and the biggest regression.
Give me a monthly scorecard and 3 goals for next month.

Sleep Analysis

Prompts focused on sleep consistency, bedtime patterns, and optimization.

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
For the last 14 days, summarize my sleep consistency. Flag nights with missing sleep data.
Give me 3 high-confidence changes to improve consistency.

Sleep and workout correlation

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
Compare my sleep data with my workout data for the last 14 days.
Does late-evening exercise (after 7 PM) correlate with lower sleep duration or later bedtimes?
What is my optimal bedtime window based on my wake-up consistency?

Recovery (HRV / Resting HR)

Track your recovery trend using heart rate variability and resting heart rate data.

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
For the last 14 days, summarize my recovery trend using HRV (if available) and resting heart rate.
Call out any anomaly days and plausible causes (sleep, workouts, low activity, missing data).

Recovery readiness check

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
Based on my HRV and resting heart rate over the last 3 days, am I recovered enough for a hard workout today?
Compare to my 30-day baseline and give a clear yes/no recommendation with reasoning.

Training Load

Monitor your workout volume and intensity to avoid overtraining.

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
Summarize my workouts for the last 7 days and compare to my baseline.
If I'm ramping too fast, suggest a safer plan for the next 7 days.

Workout consistency report

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
For the last 30 days, how many days did I work out? What types of workouts?
Calculate my average weekly workout frequency and total active calories.
Am I trending up, down, or steady compared to the prior 30 days?

Trend Comparison

Compare different time periods to understand long-term changes in your health.

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
Compare my health metrics from the last 30 days to the 30 days before that.
Focus on: steps, sleep duration, resting heart rate, and workout frequency.
Present the comparison as a table with percentage changes.

Year-over-year comparison

Use the "health-analyzer-mac-local" skill for this request.
Compare my average daily metrics for this month vs. the same month last year.
Include steps, sleep, resting heart rate, and active energy burned.
What has improved? What has declined?

Tips for Writing Custom Prompts

You can write your own prompts beyond what is listed here. Follow these guidelines for the best results:

  • Always include the skill prefix when using OpenClaw so the request is routed to your local data.
  • Specify a time range (e.g., "last 7 days", "last 30 days", "since January 1"). Without a range, the AI may default to all available data, which can be noisy.
  • Name the metrics you care about (steps, sleep duration, HRV, resting heart rate, active energy, workout type). Being specific produces better analysis.
  • Ask for a specific output format (table, scorecard, bullet points, numbered list). This makes results easier to scan and compare over time.
  • Request actionable suggestions rather than just data summaries. Prompts that end with "give me 3 suggestions" or "what should I change?" produce more useful outputs.
  • Iterate. If the first response is too broad, follow up with a narrower question. The AI retains context within the conversation.
