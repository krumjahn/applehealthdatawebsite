Home Screen Widget

Add a Health Data Sync widget to your iPhone Home Screen for one-tap syncing. The widget shows live sync status, progress, and your last sync time -- no need to open the app.

The widget requires iOS 16 or later and works with both small and medium widget sizes.

Adding the Widget

  1. Long-press on an empty area of your iPhone Home Screen until the apps start to jiggle.
  2. Tap the + button in the top-left corner to open the widget gallery.
  3. Search for "Health Data Export" or scroll to find it in the list.
  4. Choose your preferred size -- Small or Medium.
  5. Tap "Add Widget", then position it on your Home Screen.
  6. Tap Done to save.

Widget Sizes

Small Widget

A compact widget that shows:

  • Current sync status (syncing, idle, or first sync prompt)
  • Last sync time (e.g., "1 hr ago", "2d ago")
  • Sync progress percentage when actively syncing
  • One-tap action button

Medium Widget

A wider widget with more detail:

  • Everything in the small widget, plus expanded status messages
  • Sync phase details (preparing, exporting, uploading)
  • Provider information (e.g., "Direct to Mac", "iCloud")
  • More descriptive status text with full context

Widget Actions

You can configure what happens when you tap the widget. Open the main app, go to Settings > Widget, and choose one of these actions:

Action What It Does
Quick Sync (default) Immediately starts a sync to your Mac. The fastest way to keep your data up to date.
Quick Export Exports your health data as XML and opens AirDrop or the Share Sheet, depending on your preference.
Open Sync Screen Opens the Sync tab in the app so you can review settings before starting.

Sync Status Indicators

The widget updates in real time to reflect what's happening:

Status Widget Shows
Idle "Ready to sync" with last sync time. Tap to start.
Preparing "Getting ready" -- checking permissions and settings.
Exporting "Exporting data" with a progress percentage (e.g., "30% done").
Uploading "Uploading export" with transfer progress.
Complete "Sync complete" -- your data is ready on your Mac.
Failed "Sync failed" -- tap to open the app and retry.

First-Time Setup

If you add the widget before completing your first sync, it shows a "First Sync" prompt encouraging you to start your initial sync. After your first successful sync, the widget automatically switches to showing your sync status and last sync time.

Export Settings

When using the Quick Export action, you can configure two additional preferences in the app:

  • Export range: Choose between the last 90 days or all-time data.
  • Delivery method: Choose AirDrop (sends directly to a nearby device) or Share Sheet (lets you choose any sharing destination).

How Often Does the Widget Update?

The widget refreshes approximately every 15 minutes when idle. During an active sync, it updates more frequently to show real-time progress. The widget uses shared app storage to display the most current sync state without consuming significant battery.

Troubleshooting

Widget shows "First Sync" even after syncing

  • Make sure you completed a full sync (not just opened the sync screen).
  • Wait up to 15 minutes for the widget timeline to refresh.
  • Remove and re-add the widget if it remains stuck.

Widget not updating during sync

  • Widget updates rely on iOS timeline refresh. The progress may lag slightly behind the actual sync.
  • Open the main app to see real-time sync progress.

Widget not appearing in the widget gallery

  • Make sure the app is installed and has been opened at least once.
  • Restart your iPhone if the widget doesn't appear after installation.
  • Check that you're running iOS 16 or later.
