Apple Health Widget:
One-Tap Sync from Your Home Screen

Skip the app. Add a sync widget to your iPhone Home Screen and keep your Apple Health data flowing to your Mac with a single tap. Live progress, instant export, zero friction.

Why a Health Sync Widget?

You already track your health every day. Your Apple Watch records steps, heart rate, and workouts automatically. But getting that data off your iPhone and onto your Mac for analysis usually means opening the app, navigating to the sync screen, and tapping Start.

The Health Data sync widget removes those steps. Place it on your Home Screen and sync becomes a reflex -- one tap, done. The widget shows live progress so you know exactly when your data is ready for AI analysis, CSV/JSON conversion, or OpenClaw queries.

Two Sizes, Your Choice

The widget comes in two iOS sizes to fit your Home Screen layout:

Small

Compact 2x2 widget. Shows sync status, last sync time, and a one-tap action. Perfect for a quick glance.

Medium

Wider 4x2 widget. Everything in the small widget plus expanded sync phase details, provider info, and richer status messages.

How to Add the Widget

Adding the widget takes about 30 seconds:

1

Long-Press Your Home Screen

Touch and hold an empty area until the apps start jiggling. Tap the + button in the top-left corner.

2

Search for "Health Data Export"

Type the app name in the widget gallery search bar, or scroll to find it in the list.

3

Choose Your Size

Swipe between Small and Medium, then tap "Add Widget".

4

Position and Done

Drag the widget where you want it, then tap Done. Your sync shortcut is now one tap away.

What the Widget Does

The widget isn't just a shortcut -- it's a live status dashboard for your health data pipeline:

  • Idle state: Shows "Ready to sync" with the time since your last sync (e.g., "1 hr ago", "2d ago"). Tap to start a new sync.
  • Syncing: Displays the current phase (preparing, exporting, uploading) with a real-time progress percentage like "Exporting data -- 30% done".
  • Complete: Confirms "Sync complete" so you know your Mac has the latest data.
  • Failed: Shows "Sync failed" with a tap-to-retry action that opens the app.

Three Tap Actions

Configure what happens when you tap the widget. Open the app and go to Settings > Widget to choose:

Quick Sync (Default)

Immediately starts syncing your Apple Health data to your paired Mac over WiFi. The fastest path from raw data to analysis.

Quick Export

Exports your health data as XML and sends it via AirDrop or the Share Sheet. Choose between the last 90 days or all-time data.

Open Sync Screen

Opens the Sync tab so you can review settings, select specific health categories, or change your sync provider before starting.

Privacy First

The widget communicates with the main app through on-device App Group storage. Your health data never leaves your iPhone except during a sync to your own Mac on your local network. There are no servers, no cloud, and no tracking.

All sync data travels directly between your iPhone and Mac. The widget itself stores only sync status metadata -- never your actual health records.

When to Use the Widget

  • Morning routine: Tap the widget after waking up to sync overnight sleep and heart rate data to your Mac for your OpenClaw daily brief.
  • Post-workout: Sync immediately after a workout so your Mac has the latest exercise data for AI analysis.
  • Weekly exports: Use Quick Export to share a health data file with your doctor or personal trainer.
  • Data pipeline: Keep your Mac's local health database always current for API integrations and automation workflows.

Requirements

  • iOS 16 or later (WidgetKit support)
  • Health Data Export & AI Analyzer installed on your iPhone
  • For sync: the Mac app installed and both devices on the same network
